I N T R O D U C T I O N

Scientists have long predicted the changes that are apparent today, such as sea level rise, melting glaciers, and the loss of sea ice. It is now predicted that global warming will only continue through the rest of this century and beyond, manifesting as rising sea levels by 1 - 8 feet, more intense hurricanes, longer wildfire seasons, more droughts and heat waves, and all the ice melting from the arctic (NASA). The fact is, “the effects of human-caused global warming are happening now, are irreversible on the timescale of people alive today, and will worsen in the decades to come” (NASA). It is entirely dependent on future human activities to reduce the amount of carbon emissions in an attempt to avoid the worst effects of climate change. This website contains different resources for individuals to take action, learn more, and contact important government officials about supporting environment- focused policies. Remember that the most important step we can take is to spread awareness, so share this resource and keep talking about protecting our planet!
